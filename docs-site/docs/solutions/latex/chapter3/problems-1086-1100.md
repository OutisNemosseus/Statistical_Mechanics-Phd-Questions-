# Problems 1086-1100: Phase Transitions

Solutions for problems on Clausius-Clapeyron equation and phase equilibria.

## Problem 1086

**Topic:** Clausius-Clapeyron Equation

### Derivation

At phase equilibrium: $G^{(1)} = G^{(2)}$

Along coexistence curve:
$$
dG^{(1)} = dG^{(2)}
$$
$$
-S^{(1)}dT + V^{(1)}dP = -S^{(2)}dT + V^{(2)}dP
$$

$$
\boxed{\frac{dP}{dT} = \frac{S^{(2)} - S^{(1)}}{V^{(2)} - V^{(1)}} = \frac{\Delta S}{\Delta V} = \frac{L}{T\Delta V}}
$$

## Problem 1087

**Topic:** Liquid-Vapor Equilibrium

### Approximations

1. $V_{vapor} \gg V_{liquid}$
2. Ideal gas: $V = RT/P$

$$
\frac{dP}{dT} = \frac{LP}{RT^2}
$$

$$
\frac{d\ln P}{dT} = \frac{L}{RT^2}
$$

### Integrated Form (constant $L$)

$$
\ln\frac{P_2}{P_1} = -\frac{L}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T_2} - \frac{1}{T_1}\right)
$$

## Problem 1088

**Topic:** Boiling Point Elevation

### Example Problem

Vapor pressure of water at 100°C is 1 atm, $L_{vap} = 2260$ kJ/kg.

At what temperature does water boil at 0.5 atm?

### Solution

$$
\ln\frac{0.5}{1} = -\frac{2260 \times 18}{8.314}\left(\frac{1}{T} - \frac{1}{373}\right)
$$

Solving: $T \approx 354$ K = 81°C

## Problem 1089

**Topic:** Solid-Liquid Equilibrium

### Ice-Water Example

For water: $\Delta V < 0$ (ice less dense)

$$
\frac{dP}{dT} = \frac{L_{fus}}{T\Delta V} < 0
$$

Pressure increase → melting point decreases (explains ice skating).

## Problem 1090

**Topic:** Triple Point

### Conditions

Three phases coexist: $G^{(s)} = G^{(l)} = G^{(g)}$

Unique point in P-T diagram.

For water: $T_{tp} = 273.16$ K, $P_{tp} = 611.7$ Pa

## Problem 1093

**Topic:** Latent Heat Variation

### Clausius-Clapeyron with Variable L

$$
L = L_0 + \Delta C_P(T - T_0)
$$

Kirchhoff equation:
$$
\frac{dL}{dT} = \Delta C_P
$$
