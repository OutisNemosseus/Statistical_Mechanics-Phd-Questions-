# Problems 1041-1050: Heat Engine Cycles

Solutions for problems on Otto, Diesel, and other thermodynamic cycles.

## Problem 1041

**Topic:** Otto Cycle

### The Four Strokes

1. **Isentropic compression**: $1 \to 2$
2. **Isochoric heat addition**: $2 \to 3$
3. **Isentropic expansion**: $3 \to 4$
4. **Isochoric heat rejection**: $4 \to 1$

### Efficiency

$$
\eta_{Otto} = 1 - \frac{1}{r^{\gamma-1}}
$$

where $r = V_1/V_2$ is the compression ratio.

For $r = 8$, $\gamma = 1.4$:
$$
\eta = 1 - \frac{1}{8^{0.4}} = 0.565 = 56.5\%
$$

## Problem 1042

**Topic:** Diesel Cycle

### The Four Stages

1. **Isentropic compression**: $1 \to 2$
2. **Isobaric heat addition**: $2 \to 3$
3. **Isentropic expansion**: $3 \to 4$
4. **Isochoric heat rejection**: $4 \to 1$

### Efficiency

$$
\eta_{Diesel} = 1 - \frac{1}{r^{\gamma-1}} \cdot \frac{r_c^\gamma - 1}{\gamma(r_c - 1)}
$$

where $r_c = V_3/V_2$ is the cutoff ratio.

---

## Problem 1043

**Topic:** Stirling Cycle

### Key Feature

Two isothermal and two isochoric processes with regenerator.

Theoretical efficiency = Carnot efficiency:
$$
\eta_{Stirling} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}
$$

---

## Problem 1044

**Topic:** Brayton Cycle (Gas Turbine)

### Efficiency

$$
\eta_{Brayton} = 1 - \frac{1}{r_p^{(\gamma-1)/\gamma}}
$$

where $r_p = P_2/P_1$ is the pressure ratio.

---

## Problem 1045

**Topic:** Rankine Cycle (Steam Power)

### Key Stages

1. Isentropic compression (pump)
2. Isobaric heat addition (boiler)
3. Isentropic expansion (turbine)
4. Isobaric heat rejection (condenser)

---

## Problem 1046

**Topic:** Refrigeration Cycle

### Coefficient of Performance

$$
COP = \frac{Q_C}{W} = \frac{h_1 - h_4}{h_2 - h_1}
$$

Using refrigerant enthalpy values.

---

## Problem 1047

**Topic:** Heat Pump vs Resistance Heating

### Comparison

Heat pump delivers more heat per unit energy:
$$
\frac{Q_{HP}}{W} = COP_{HP} > 1
$$

Resistance heating: $Q = W$ (COP = 1)

---

## Problem 1048

**Topic:** Combined Cycle

### Gas + Steam Turbine

Overall efficiency:
$$
\eta_{combined} = \eta_{gas} + \eta_{steam}(1 - \eta_{gas})
$$

Can exceed 60% in modern plants.

---

## Problem 1049

**Topic:** Mean Effective Pressure

### Definition

$$
MEP = \frac{W_{net}}{V_1 - V_2}
$$

Relates cycle work to engine displacement.

---

## Problem 1050

**Topic:** Engine Power Output

### Calculation

$$
P = \frac{W_{cycle} \cdot N}{n}
$$

where $N$ = rotational speed, $n$ = number of revolutions per cycle.
