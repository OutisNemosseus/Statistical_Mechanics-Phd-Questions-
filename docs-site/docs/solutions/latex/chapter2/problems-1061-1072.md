# Problems 1061-1072: Free Expansion & Irreversibility

Solutions for problems on free expansion, Joule-Thomson effect, and irreversible processes.

## Problem 1061

**Topic:** Free Expansion of Ideal Gas

### Setup

Gas expands from $V_i$ to $V_f$ into vacuum.

### Analysis

- Work: $W = 0$ (no external pressure)
- Heat: $Q = 0$ (adiabatic)
- Internal energy: $\Delta U = 0$
- Temperature: $\Delta T = 0$ (ideal gas)

### Entropy Change

$$
\Delta S_{gas} = nR\ln\frac{V_f}{V_i} > 0
$$

Since $\Delta S_{surroundings} = 0$:
$$
\Delta S_{universe} = nR\ln\frac{V_f}{V_i} > 0
$$

Process is **irreversible**.

## Problem 1062

**Topic:** Entropy of Free Expansion

### Why Does Entropy Increase?

Even though $Q = 0$:
$$
\Delta S = \int\frac{\delta Q_{rev}}{T} \neq \int\frac{\delta Q_{actual}}{T}
$$

Must calculate via reversible path (isothermal expansion).

## Problem 1063

**Topic:** Joule Experiment

### Key Result

For ideal gas: $\left(\frac{\partial U}{\partial V}\right)_T = 0$

For real gas: $\left(\frac{\partial U}{\partial V}\right)_T = \frac{a}{V^2}$ (van der Waals)

---

## Problem 1064

**Topic:** Joule-Thomson Effect

### Throttling Process

Gas forced through porous plug at constant enthalpy.

### Joule-Thomson Coefficient

$$
\mu_{JT} = \left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial P}\right)_H = \frac{1}{C_P}\left[T\left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial T}\right)_P - V\right]
$$

For ideal gas: $\mu_{JT} = 0$

For van der Waals gas:
$$
\mu_{JT} = \frac{1}{C_P}\left(\frac{2a}{RT} - b\right)
$$

---

## Problem 1065

**Topic:** Inversion Temperature

### Definition

Temperature where $\mu_{JT} = 0$:
$$
T_i = \frac{2a}{Rb}
$$

- $T < T_i$: Cooling on expansion ($\mu_{JT} > 0$)
- $T > T_i$: Heating on expansion ($\mu_{JT} < 0$)

---

## Problem 1066

**Topic:** Liquefaction of Gases

### Linde Process

Uses Joule-Thomson effect for cooling:
1. Compress gas
2. Cool at constant pressure
3. Throttle (isenthalpic expansion)
4. Repeat

Requires starting below inversion temperature.

---

## Problem 1067

**Topic:** Irreversible Heat Transfer

### Entropy Production

Heat $Q$ flows from $T_1$ to $T_2$ ($T_1 > T_2$):
$$
\Delta S_{prod} = Q\left(\frac{1}{T_2} - \frac{1}{T_1}\right) > 0
$$

---

## Problem 1068

**Topic:** Irreversible Mixing

### Mixing at Different Temperatures

Two bodies at $T_1$ and $T_2$ reach equilibrium at $T_f$:
$$
\Delta S = mC\ln\frac{T_f^2}{T_1 T_2}
$$

Maximum when $T_f = \sqrt{T_1 T_2}$ (geometric mean).

---

## Problem 1069

**Topic:** Available Work

### Exergy Analysis

Maximum useful work from system at $T$ with environment at $T_0$:
$$
W_{max} = (U - U_0) - T_0(S - S_0) + P_0(V - V_0)
$$

## Problem 1070

**Topic:** Entropy and Information

### Boltzmann Entropy

$$
S = k_B\ln\Omega
$$

Connection to information theory: $S \propto$ missing information about microstate.

## Problem 1071-1072

**Topic:** Advanced Irreversibility Problems

### General Principle

For any irreversible process:
$$
\Delta S_{universe} = \Delta S_{system} + \Delta S_{surroundings} > 0
$$
