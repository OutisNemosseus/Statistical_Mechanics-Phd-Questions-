# Problems 1021-1030: Adiabatic Processes & Radiation

Solutions for problems on adiabatic processes, sound propagation, and thermal radiation.

## Problem 1021

**Topic:** Adiabatic Compression

Air ($\gamma = 1.4$) at 300 K and 1 atm is compressed adiabatically to half its volume.

### Solution

Using $TV^{\gamma-1} = \text{const}$:

$$
T_f = T_i \left(\frac{V_i}{V_f}\right)^{\gamma-1} = 300 \times 2^{0.4} = 396 \text{ K}
$$

Using $PV^\gamma = \text{const}$:

$$
P_f = P_i \left(\frac{V_i}{V_f}\right)^\gamma = 1 \times 2^{1.4} = 2.64 \text{ atm}
$$

## Problem 1022

**Topic:** Adiabatic Expansion of Steam

### Key Concepts

For adiabatic expansion:
$$
W = \frac{P_1V_1 - P_2V_2}{\gamma - 1} = \frac{nR(T_1 - T_2)}{\gamma - 1}
$$

For steam: $\gamma \approx 1.3$

## Problem 1023

**Topic:** Speed of Sound

### Derivation

Sound propagates adiabatically (too fast for heat exchange):

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma P}{\rho}} = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma RT}{M}}
$$

For air at 300 K:
$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{1.4 \times 8.314 \times 300}{0.029}} = 347 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Problem 1024

**Topic:** Adiabatic Atmosphere

### Adiabatic Lapse Rate

$$
\frac{dT}{dz} = -\frac{g}{C_P}
$$

For dry air:
$$
\Gamma = \frac{9.8}{1005} \approx 9.8 \text{ K/km}
$$

## Problem 1025

**Topic:** Atmospheric Pressure vs Altitude

### Solution

From hydrostatic equation and adiabatic condition:
$$
P = P_0\left(1 - \frac{\Gamma z}{T_0}\right)^{g/R\Gamma}
$$

## Problem 1026

**Topic:** Stefan-Boltzmann Law

### Key Equations

Power radiated by blackbody:
$$
P = \sigma A T^4
$$

where $\sigma = 5.67 \times 10^{-8}$ W/(m²·K⁴)

For non-blackbody with emissivity $\varepsilon$:
$$
P = \varepsilon \sigma A T^4
$$

---

## Problem 1027

**Topic:** Thermal Equilibrium with Radiation

### Solution Method

At equilibrium, power in = power out:
$$
P_{absorbed} = P_{radiated}
$$

$$
\alpha \cdot P_{incident} = \varepsilon \sigma A T^4
$$

For blackbody: $\alpha = \varepsilon = 1$

## Problem 1028

**Topic:** Blackbody Sphere

A blackbody sphere of radius 10 cm at 1000 K.

### Solution

Surface area: $A = 4\pi r^2 = 4\pi(0.1)^2 = 0.126$ m²

Power radiated:
$$
P = \sigma A T^4 = 5.67 \times 10^{-8} \times 0.126 \times 1000^4 = 7140 \text{ W}
$$

Wien's law for peak wavelength:
$$
\lambda_{max} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{T} = 2.9 \text{ μm}
$$

---

## Problem 1029

**Topic:** Cooling by Radiation

### Newton's Law of Cooling (Radiation)

For small temperature differences:
$$
\frac{dT}{dt} = -\frac{4\sigma A T_0^3}{mC}(T - T_0)
$$

Time constant: $\tau = \frac{mC}{4\sigma A T_0^3}$

---

## Problem 1030

**Topic:** Solar Constant

### Solution

Solar constant $S \approx 1370$ W/m²

Sun's surface temperature from:
$$
S = \sigma T_{sun}^4 \left(\frac{R_{sun}}{d_{sun}}\right)^2
$$

$$
T_{sun} = \left(\frac{S \cdot d_{sun}^2}{\sigma R_{sun}^2}\right)^{1/4} \approx 5780 \text{ K}
$$
